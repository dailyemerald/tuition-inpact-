The Cost

Seven in every 10 college graduates had loan debt in 2013, the Institute for College Access & Success reports.

Their average cost: $28,400.

In 2014, half of UO’s graduating class had debt of their own.

Their average cost: $24,508.

Next year, tuition and fees will rise about 3.7 percent for UO students — up to $32,023.50 for out of state students and $10,288.50 for Oregon residents.

About a quarter of UO’s students are designated as “high need” students because they’re eligible for a Federal Pell grant — this typically means their parents make under $50,000 per year.

But the most they can receive in aid next year is $5,775 — which leaves a significant cost to be repaid.
